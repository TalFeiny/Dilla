EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

March 2026 marks a strong close to a transformative trailing twelve-month period for Apex Risk Partners. Total revenue for the TTM period reached $24.2M, representing year-over-year growth of approximately 35%. The successful integration of Harbor Insurance Group and Summit Specialty Brokers has significantly expanded our platform capabilities across P&C, employee benefits, and specialty lines.

Adjusted EBITDA for the TTM period was $4.70M (19.4% margin), reflecting improved operating leverage as integration synergies begin to materialize. We remain comfortable within all financial covenant thresholds, with our total leverage ratio at 5.52x against a 6.0x maximum.

________________________________________________________________________________

REVENUE PERFORMANCE

TTM revenue of $24.2M reflects strong execution across all business lines:

  *Property & Casualty commissions grew 38% year-over-year, driven by both organic expansion and the Harbor and Summit platforms
  *Employee Benefits commissions increased 28%, with our platform benefits practice showing particular strength in the mid-market segment
  *Specialty Lines revenue nearly doubled, reflecting the Summit acquisition's immediate contribution to our specialty capabilities
  *Contingent commissions of $1.3M reflect our continued strong carrier relationships and disciplined underwriting selection

Organic growth momentum continues to build. Q1 2026 (Jan-Mar) revenue of $6.19M compares favorably to Q2 2025 (Apr-Jun) revenue of $5.48M, demonstrating the positive trajectory of our combined platform. We expect organic growth to accelerate in the second half of 2026 as cross-selling initiatives between the legacy platform and acquired books begin to gain traction.

Our fee income of $524K for the TTM reflects a deliberate shift toward advisory services, which carry higher margins than traditional commission business.

________________________________________________________________________________

PROFITABILITY

Adjusted EBITDA of $4.70M represents a meaningful improvement from prior year levels. Key adjustments to reported EBITDA include:

  *M&A advisory fees: $75,680 (one-time, related to Summit transaction close)
  *Integration consulting: $349,160 (one-time, winding down as integrations complete)
  *System migration costs: $177,260 (one-time, will conclude by Q3 2026)
  *Non-recurring legal costs: $67,120 (transaction-related legal above run-rate)
  *Year-end management transition bonus: $48,280 (one-time retention payment)
  *Non-recurring recruiting fees: $42,500 (acquisition-related hiring)

Total add-backs: $860,000

We are on track for these costs to decline materially in the second half of 2026 as both integrations reach completion.

Gross margin for the TTM was approximately 56%, consistent with industry benchmarks for diversified insurance brokerages. We have maintained disciplined cost management while investing in the talent and technology infrastructure needed to support our growth trajectory.
